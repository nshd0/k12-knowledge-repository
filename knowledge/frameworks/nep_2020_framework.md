# National Education Policy (NEP) 2020 Framework

## Overview
The National Education Policy 2020 is India's comprehensive education reform framework, approved by the Union Cabinet on July 29, 2020. It replaces the previous National Policy on Education, 1986.

## Vision
Transform India into a global knowledge superpower by making both school and higher education more holistic, flexible, multidisciplinary, suited to 21st-century needs, and aimed at bringing out the unique capabilities of each student.

## Key Principles

### 1. Universal Access
- 100% Gross Enrolment Ratio (GER) from pre-primary to secondary level by 2030
- Special emphasis on Socially and Economically Disadvantaged Groups (SEDGs)
- Focus on out-of-school children through open schooling

### 2. Foundational Literacy and Numeracy
- National Mission on Foundational Literacy and Numeracy
- All children to attain foundational literacy and numeracy by Grade 3 (by 2025)
- Focus on early childhood care and education (ECCE)

### 3. Holistic and Integrated Learning
- Multi-disciplinary approach
- Integration of vocational education
- Emphasis on experiential learning
- Arts, sports, and fitness integrated into curriculum

## Pedagogical Structure: 5+3+3+4 Design

Replacing the 10+2 structure with a new 5+3+3+4 curricular structure:

### Foundational Stage (Ages 3-8) - 5 Years
- **Duration**: 3 years of Anganwadi/pre-school + 2 years (Grades 1-2)
- **Focus**: Play-based, activity-based learning
- **Key Areas**: 
  - Cognitive development
  - Motor skills
  - Socio-emotional development
  - Early literacy and numeracy
- **Pedagogy**: Learning through play, discovery, activity-based

### Preparatory Stage (Ages 8-11) - 3 Years
- **Duration**: Grades 3-5
- **Focus**: Transition from play-based to formal learning
- **Key Areas**:
  - Reading and writing skills
  - Number sense and mathematical reasoning
  - Scientific temper
  - Arts and crafts
- **Pedagogy**: Interactive classroom learning, textbooks introduced

### Middle Stage (Ages 11-14) - 3 Years
- **Duration**: Grades 6-8
- **Focus**: Subject-oriented learning with abstract concepts
- **Key Areas**:
  - Sciences, mathematics, arts, social sciences, humanities
  - Exposure to vocational crafts
  - Introduction to coding
- **Pedagogy**: Experiential learning in sciences, mathematics, arts
- **New Feature**: Students learn at least one vocational skill

### Secondary Stage (Ages 14-18) - 4 Years
- **Duration**: Grades 9-12
- **Focus**: Multidisciplinary study, no rigid stream separation
- **Key Features**:
  - Flexible subject choices
  - Choice between academic and vocational streams
  - Semester/modular system
  - Multiple entry/exit options with certificates
- **Pedagogy**: Critical thinking, project-based learning, research skills

## Assessment Reforms

### 1. Competency-Based Assessment
- Shift from summative to formative assessment
- 360-degree holistic progress card
- Focus on learning outcomes

### 2. Board Exams
- Board exams to test core competencies and essential concepts
- Students can attempt board exams twice per year
- Reduction in exam stress

### 3. Assessment Tools
- PARAKH (National Assessment Centre) for standard-setting
- State Census Exams (Class 3, 5, 8)
- National Achievement Survey
- International assessments (PISA participation)

## Curriculum and Pedagogy

### 1. National Curriculum Framework (NCF)
- New NCF to be developed by NCERT
- Based on NEP 2020 principles
- Aligned with Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs)

### 2. Languages
- **Three-Language Formula**: Mother tongue/local language, English, and one additional Indian language
- Home language/mother tongue as medium of instruction until Grade 5, preferably till Grade 8
- No language imposed
- Classical languages offered at all levels
- Foreign languages offered in secondary stage

### 3. Content Integration
- Indian knowledge systems integrated
- Constitutional values and ethics
- Environmental education and sustainability
- Digital literacy and computational thinking
- Financial literacy
- Health and wellness
- Life skills and 21st-century skills

## Teacher Education

### 1. Qualifications
- 4-year integrated B.Ed. as minimum qualification by 2030
- Master's in education for teaching Grades 6-8

### 2. Professional Development
- Continuous Professional Development (CPD) mandatory
- 50 hours per year of in-service training
- Technology-enabled teacher platforms (DIKSHA)

### 3. Teacher Career Progression
- Clear career paths with promotional opportunities
- Performance-based incentives
- Merit-based appointments

## Technology in Education

### 1. Digital Infrastructure
- National Educational Technology Forum (NETF)
- Digital classrooms and smart boards
- Virtual labs

### 2. Online Learning Platforms
- DIKSHA for digital content
- SWAYAM for MOOCs
- PM eVIDYA for multi-modal access

### 3. Educational Software
- Adaptive learning platforms
- AI-powered personalized learning
- Educational apps in regional languages

## Equity and Inclusion

### 1. Special Focus Groups
- Gender Inclusion Fund
- SEDGs: SC, ST, OBC, minorities
- Students with disabilities (CWSN)
- Migrant children

### 2. Measures
- Free quality education from pre-primary to Grade 12
- Mid-day meal program extended to pre-primary
- Scholarships and financial support
- Infrastructure development in underserved areas

## Vocational Education

### 1. Integration
- Vocational education from Grade 6
- Internships with local vocational experts
- Partnerships with industry

### 2. Skill Development
- Exposure to minimum of one vocational skill
- 10-day bagless period for experiential learning
- Integration with ITIs and polytechnics

## Governance and Regulation

### 1. Institutional Changes
- Rashtriya Shiksha Aayog (RSA) as apex body
- State School Standards Authority (SSSA) for standard-setting
- Independent regulatory system

### 2. School Complexes
- Cluster-based governance
- Shared infrastructure and resources
- Integrated management

### 3. Autonomy
- Greater autonomy to schools
- School Management Committees (SMCs) empowered
- Community participation

## Adult Literacy

### 1. Target
- 100% adult literacy by 2030
- Basic education equivalent to Grade 3

### 2. Approach
- Volunteers and peer learning
- Technology-enabled programs
- Integration with skill development

## Implementation Timeline

### Short-term (2020-2025)
- Foundational Literacy and Numeracy Mission
- NCF development
- Teacher training programs
- Infrastructure upgrades

### Medium-term (2025-2030)
- Full implementation of 5+3+3+4 structure
- Universal GER achievement
- Vocational education integration

### Long-term (2030-2040)
- India as knowledge superpower
- World-class research universities
- 100% literacy

- Number sense up to 99
- Basic problem-solving
- Gross and fine motor skills
- Social skills and emotional regulation

### Preparatory Stage

- Basic operations in mathematics
- Scientific observation

- Digital literacy basics

### Middle Stage
- Abstract thinking in mathematics
- Scientific method application
- Research skills

### Secondary Stage
- Advanced disciplinary knowledge
- Independent research capability
- Vocational expertise
- College/career readiness
- 21st-century competencies
